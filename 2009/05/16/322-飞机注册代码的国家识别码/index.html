Airfield ICAO Codes and Aircraft Registration prefixes by Country

The data below shows the ICAO airfield codes and Aircraft Prefixes for each country. A brief description of each data column is appended below.



Airfield ICAO

 

Every airport ICAO code is a four letter group.
Only the letters that uniquely identify each country are only shown here
- e.g. EG for the UK or K for the USA.
The remaining letters identify individual airports and are not included.

Region


AFR-  Africa
EUR - Europe
NAM - North America
CAM - Central America
SAM - South America
MID -  Middle East
FAE - Far East
PAC - Pacific

This is a rough grouping only. I use it to organise the airlines in FS2004.
 

Registration Prefix


The letters/numbers that are unique to each country.
These are sometimes separated by a hyphen on aircraft e.g. PH-MAR
 

Registration Suffix


The remaining registration code unique to each aircraft.
These can be letters or numbers - or a combination of both.

The suffix identifies how many characters are used and of which type.

e.g. 123ab indicates three numbers and two letters follow the registration prefix.

Note: The Laos and Panama registrations are too long for FS2004.
